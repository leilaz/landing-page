Why we read Wikipedia

Understanding why people read Wikipedia

Project overview

Every second, 6000 people view Wikipedia pages from across the globe and we don’t know why. In this research, we aim to characterize the behavior of Wikipedia readers and to learn about the needs and motivations behind Wikipedia pageviews.

The first part of this research focused on building a taxonomy of Wikipedia readers. Through a series of surveys ran on English, Persian, and Spanish Wikipedias we built a robust taxonomy of Wikipedia readers along three axes: motivation, depth of information need, and prior familiarity with the topic read. We ran a large scale survey in English Wikipedia in 2016 and studied the prevalence of Wikipedia use-cases using this taxonomy. We also did deeper characterization of English Wikipedia readers by combining the results of the survey with Wikipedia’s HTTP webrequest logs. You can learn more about the result of that study by reading our full paper or listening to one of our presentations.

More recently, we ran a survey using the taxonomy we built in the first part of this research, this time in 14 Wikipedia languages, to test the robustness of the results of the first study and learn about Wikipedia readers across a broader spectrum of languages. More than 250,000 responses are submitted as part of the surveys run across these languages and we are now analyzing the results. (Ongoing documentation)
